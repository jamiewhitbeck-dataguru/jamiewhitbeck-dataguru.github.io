Selected Projects

Document Storage Application

A fun personal project and Python GUI learning exercise. A friend needed a local, searchable database for storing scraped text—no web apps, no cloud, no fuss. I built a standalone SQLite-backed desktop application with a full GUI that handled input, classification, search, and display far beyond what he expected. He will never lose his documentation on canning squirrels and proper roadkill preparation again.

Mobile Application List Pagination Procedure

While supporting development of a mobile CMMS app as the Lead SQL Developer, I was asked to optimize a stored procedure used for paginating item lists. Execution times averaged 27 seconds—clearly unacceptable. I discovered the developer planned to replicate this procedure over 300 times, once per module. I immediately saw the impending development and maintenance nightmares. I designed a single, dynamic, parameterized procedure that eliminated the redundancy and slashed execution times to under 20 milliseconds—a 99.94% improvement.

Scripted SaaS Database Migration

Most companies brace for all-nighters and prayer circles when it's time to migrate or consolidate SQL environments. I wrote a reusable stored procedure, deployed it to our container registration database, and successfully migrated more than 500 databases across three legacy servers to a single SSD-powered instance in under 1 hour. With automation, linked servers, testing, and full logging, the average impact was less than 10 seconds per tenant.

User Data Encryption

As the DBA for a CMMS vendor with over 1,000 tenant databases and thousands of on-premises clients, I discovered that the "encryption" used for user credentials was laughably insecure—especially considering some clients were government agencies, hospitals, and critical infrastructure organizations, many of which used our GIS module to map their assets. I built a robust, standards-compliant solution to secure all credentials, resolving a gaping vulnerability before it became a real incident.
